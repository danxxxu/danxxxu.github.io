Hello,

Dan here. I am a creative researcher and maker in the play field of art, science and technology. I often take and combine inspirations and insights from different disciplines to explore questions and phenomena that trigger my curiosity. Among other things, I am particularly interested in the poetics and aesthetics of interaction and communication through and with computational technologies, as well as how they shape and mediate our embodied and social experience.

I am currently working on my PhD in the field of interactive art at Leiden University in the Netherlands. My research explores co-located interaction in interactive art: when more than one co-located audience interact with the art system at the same time and the interaction between them is integral to the artwork. I am interested in what forms can co-located interaction take and, more importantly, what makes it interactive.

Prior to the PhD, I worked as a Digital Transformation Designer at Digital Society School affiliated with Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences. It is a combination of co-creating, researching, teaching and coaching. In this period, I have led and worked on digital transformation projects that questions the user engagement and empowerment, the emergence of meaningful connection, the future of forensic health care and inclusive collective experience.

I hold a MSc in Media Technology from Leiden University and a BSc in Applied Physics from Beijing Institute of Technology. For an overview of my experiences, please check my CV. For other projects and prototypes I have created (often in collaboration with other bright minds) and tinkered with, please check my portfolio or Youtube.
