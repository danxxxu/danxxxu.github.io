Hello, my name is Dan Xu. I am a creative researcher and maker/coder in the playground of art, science and technology. Among many things, I am particularly interested in the poetics and creative possibilities of human (and non-human) computer interaction, as well as how such technologies shape and mediate our embodied and social realities. Coming from a multidisciplinary background, I like to take and combine insights and approaches from different disciplines to explore questions and phenomena that trigger my curiosity. Besides research, I often make stuff with sound, text and code.

Currently, I am mainly busy with my PhD research at Leiden University in the Netherlands. In this project, I am exploring different ways to think about and create interactions in the context of interactive art. One of the main outcomes of this research is a web-based tool for modelling, visualising and creating interactive dialogues. Using the tool as an analytical lens, I have peered into the fields of co-located audience interaction and more-than-human interactive art that involved both human and non-human participants. All my publications can be found here.

Prior to the PhD, I worked as a Digital Transformation Designer at Digital Society School affiliated with Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences. In this period, I have led and worked on digital transformation projects that questions the user engagement and empowerment, the emergence of meaningful connection, the future of forensic health care and inclusive collective experience.

I hold a MSc in Media Technology from Leiden University and a BSc in Applied Physics from Beijing Institute of Technology. For a detailed overview of my experiences, please check my CV. You can find other projects and prototypes I have created (often in collaboration with other bright minds) and tinkered with in my Youtube.
